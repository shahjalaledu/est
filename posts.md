## Understanding Browser Readability
When you visit a website, your browser scans the HTML code. It looks for specific tags like `<article>`, `<h1>`, and `<p>`. But more importantly, it counts the words. If a page has fewer than 300 words, the browser assumes it is not worth reading aloud and hides the listen button.
